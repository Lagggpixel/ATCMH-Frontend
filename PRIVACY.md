# 1. Introduction

ATC Mentorship Hub (“ATCMH”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) operates an online mentorship platform for air traffic control-related mentoring, session organisation, testing, activity tracking, and related community administration.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect personal information when you use:
- The ATC Mentorship Hub website
- The Dashboard and Exams Center
- The ATCMH Discord bot
- Related dashboards, moderation tools, training tools, and administrative features

Before logging in to the Dashboard or Exams Center, you must affirmatively agree to the Terms of Service and acknowledge that your information will be handled as described in this Privacy Policy. ATCMH records that acceptance after your login provider verifies your identity and before it issues an application session. Interacting with the Discord bot, submitting mentorship requests, or participating in other ATCMH-managed processes also involves the data handling described here.

# 2. Information We Collect

We only collect information that is reasonably necessary to operate ATCMH, provide mentorship features, protect the platform, and maintain mentorship records.

## 2.1 Account and Profile Information

Depending on how you use ATCMH, we may collect:
- Discord user ID
- Discord username, display name, avatar, and server nickname
- Central authentication information and secure opaque session identifiers for the Dashboard and Exams Center
- Infinite Flight Community username, user ID, or related profile identifiers
- Linked Discord and Infinite Flight Community identities, link status, conflicts, and account-link history
- Organisation, role, rank, membership, staff status, or permission level within ATCMH
- Policy-acceptance evidence, including the accepted document versions and hashes, account, accepting application, timestamp, and login IP address

## 2.2 Mentorship Information

To operate the mentorship system, we may collect and store:
- mentorship applications and requests
- mentor, mentee, recruiter, or staff assignments
- session attendance and mentorship history
- session notes, feedback, outcomes, and progression status
- exam attempts, submitted answers, scores, overrides, and pass/fail outcomes
- training case records
- activity records, including mentorship threads and mentorship sessions
- administrative decisions, such as approvals, rejections, cancellations, warnings, or status changes

This information may be visible to authorised staff where needed to run the mentorship programme.

## 2.3 Discord Bot Interaction Data

When you interact with the ATCMH Discord bot, or when the bot is used in a server where it is installed, we may process:
- slash command usage
- command parameters
- button, menu, modal, or interaction responses
- timestamps of interactions
- bot-generated logs
- moderation, training, session, or activity events
- message content only where necessary for a feature and only where the bot has access to that content

The bot does not intentionally collect messages from channels it cannot access. The bot is not designed to record voice calls, video calls, or private conversations unless a specific feature is clearly enabled and disclosed.

## 2.4 Website Usage and Technical Data

When you use the website, Dashboard, Exams Center, or an authentication flow, we may collect technical information such as:
- IP address, including the login IP address for each authentication lifecycle request
- browser type and version
- device type
- operating system
- user agent
- referring page
- pages viewed
- request timestamps
- login timestamps
- error logs
- security and audit logs

Authentication lifecycle records may include a unique transaction or request identifier, application, provider, outcome, linked account, timestamps, and trusted proxy or network information. ATCMH may compare exact normalised login IP addresses shared by accounts and historical Discord-to-Infinite-Flight link conflicts to create candidates for alternate-account evidence review. These candidates support human review; they do not automatically prove misconduct or trigger a merge, suspension, or other penalty.

This information is used to keep the platform secure, diagnose issues, prevent abuse, improve reliability, and support the legal, safety, and abuse-prevention purposes described in this Policy.

## 2.5 Cookies and Local Storage

The website may use cookies or similar technologies for:
- login sessions
- authentication security
- remembering user preferences
- protecting against unauthorised access

Dashboard and Exams Center authentication uses ATCMH-issued opaque session cookies. Provider access tokens and identity claims are not intentionally placed in browser-readable login cookies or browser query parameters.

You may be able to disable cookies in your browser, but some website features may stop working correctly.

## 2.6 User-Submitted Content

We may collect content you submit through the website or bot, including:
- forms
- comments
- files or attachments

You should not submit sensitive personal information unless it is necessary for the specific request.

# 3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to:
- create and manage user accounts;
- authenticate users;
- link verified Discord and Infinite Flight Community identities to a canonical ATCMH account;
- record and enforce acceptance of the current Terms of Service and Privacy Policy;
- operate the ATCMH website and Discord bot;
- manage mentorship, training, exams, and progression;
- assign mentors or staff;
- track session attendance and mentorship history;
- process applications, requests and exams;
- display relevant information to authorised staff and users;
- enforce permissions and role-based access;
- prevent spam, abuse, fraud, unauthorised access, and misuse;
- investigate technical issues, security incidents, or rule violations;
- identify shared-IP or historical identity-link evidence for manual alternate-account review;
- improve the platform and user experience;
- communicate important updates;
- comply with legal, safety, or platform obligations.

We do not sell your personal information.

# 4. Legal Basis for Processing

Where data protection laws require a lawful basis, we rely on one or more of the following:

Contract or service necessity: to provide the ATCMH website, bot, mentorship tools, accounts, and related services.
Legitimate interests: to operate, secure, improve, and administer the platform; manage training; prevent abuse; and maintain accurate activity records.
Consent: where we ask for specific permission, such as optional features, optional communications, or non-essential cookies.
Legal obligation: where we must retain, disclose, or process information to comply with applicable laws or valid requests.

# 5. How Information Is Shared

We may share information only where necessary and appropriate.

## 5.1 Within ATCMH

Information may be visible to authorised users, including:
- ATCMH moderators
- ATCMH mentors
- authorised Infinite Flight or IFATC staff, where relevant
- IFATC recruiters, where relevant
- other users where the feature requires visibility, such as public leaderboards, session lists, or mentorship status displays

Access is intended to be limited based on role, permission level, and operational need.

Raw login IP addresses, provider conflict counterparts, and alternate-account evidence review are restricted to the ATCMH super administrator. Other administrative audit views may show that a login event occurred while redacting those security-sensitive details.

## 5.2 With Discord

If you use the Discord bot, Discord processes information under its own terms and privacy policy. ATCMH receives information from Discord through Discord’s API where needed for bot functionality.

## 5.3 With Service Providers

We may use third-party providers for:

- VPS infrastructure
- backups

These providers may process data on our behalf only as needed to provide their services.

## 5.4 Legal, Safety, and Abuse Prevention

We may disclose information if we reasonably believe it is necessary to:
- comply with applicable law;
- respond to valid legal requests;
- protect ATCMH, users, or the public;
- investigate abuse, security incidents, or misconduct;
- enforce platform rules, terms, or community policies.

# 6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as it is reasonably necessary to operate ATCMH, maintain mentorship records and exam history, protect the integrity of the programme, investigate abuse, resolve disputes, and comply with legal, safety, or platform obligations.

At present, ATCMH does not apply a fixed automatic deletion period to mentorship, training, exam, progression, or administrative records. These records may be retained indefinitely while ATCMH continues to operate, because they are used to maintain accurate mentorship history, prevent repeated abuse, support staff decisions, and preserve programme integrity.

We may retain:

* account and profile information while your account or ATCMH profile remains active;
* Discord identifiers and linked platform identifiers while needed to identify users within ATCMH;
* immutable account-link history and policy-acceptance evidence while needed to establish account ownership, consent, audit integrity, or resolve disputes;
* mentorship applications, session records, notes, outcomes, and progression history while needed for programme administration;
* exam attempts, submitted answers, scores, overrides, and pass/fail outcomes while needed for audit, progression, appeal, and integrity purposes;
* moderation, security, authentication, login IP, alternate-account evidence, and audit records while needed to protect ATCMH, users, and the wider community; these records may be retained indefinitely for audit integrity and abuse prevention;
* technical logs while needed for security, troubleshooting, abuse prevention, and platform maintenance;
* backup copies until they are replaced, deleted, or overwritten through normal backup processes.

Where information is no longer required in an identifiable form, we may delete, anonymise, or restrict access to it.

You may request deletion of your personal information as described in Section 7. Some information may be retained after a deletion request where retention is necessary for security, anti-abuse, audit integrity, unresolved disputes, legal obligations, or accurate historical mentorship records.

# 7. Data Deletion Requests

You may request deletion of your personal information by contacting us at: `hello@atcmh.org`

To help us process your request, include:
- your Discord user ID
- a clear description of the data you want deleted

We will review deletion requests and respond within a reasonable period.

Some data may not be deleted immediately if we need to retain it for:
- security investigations
- anti-abuse records
- unresolved disputes
- audit integrity
- legal obligations
- preventing repeated misuse
- maintaining accurate historical training records

Where full deletion is not possible, we may anonymise or restrict access to the data where appropriate.

# 9. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal information, including:
- access controls
- role-based permissions
- password hashing where passwords are used
- secure session handling
- audit logging
- server-side validation
- database access restrictions
- regular maintenance and security review where practical

No system is completely secure. You are responsible for keeping your own account credentials secure and for reporting suspected unauthorised access.

# 10. Children and Younger Users

ATCMH is not intended for users who are below the minimum age required to use Discord or the relevant online services in their country.

If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a user who is not permitted to use the service, we may delete the information and restrict access.

# 11. International Users

ATCMH may be accessed by users in different countries. Your information may be processed or stored in countries other than your own, depending on our hosting, infrastructure, and service providers.

Where required by applicable law, we will take appropriate steps to protect personal information transferred internationally.

# 12. Automated Processing

The ATCMH website and bot may automatically process information to perform routine platform functions, such as:
- assigning roles
- opening or closing mentorship cases
- recording activity
- sending reminders
- calculating progression status

We do not intend to make legally significant decisions about users solely by automated means. Important mentorship, disciplinary, or administrative decisions should be reviewed by authorised staff.
